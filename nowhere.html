Overview

Nowhere Interesting LLC is the container for everything that doesn't fit inside a job description. It started in 2018 as a way to take on branding and graphic design work alongside school and internships. It's grown into a more intentional practice that spans visual identity, futures consulting, startup advisory, and design and product workshops. Most client work is confidential. The studio is the other side of the same practice as the Microsoft work — more speculative, more personal, more willing to be weird.

What the Studio Does

Branding

Visual Identity

Brand strategy, logo design, visual systems, and identity guidelines. Primarily for independent musicians, small businesses, and early-stage startups where the brand has to do real work fast.

Graphic Design

Print & Digital

Posters, merch, editorial design, and digital assets. A lot of this work is for the independent music community — show flyers, album artwork, zines.

Futures

Consulting & Foresight

Trend mapping, scenario planning, and product futures for companies thinking about what their category looks like in 5–10 years. The spatial computing and music backgrounds make this more grounded than most futures work.

Advisory

Startup & Product

Early-stage product advisory for founders who need a PM or design perspective without a full hire. Especially useful for companies building developer tools, hardware, or community-dependent products.

01 Branding · Music

Independent Artist Identities

Visual identity systems for independent musicians across rock, electronic, and experimental music. The constraint that makes this interesting: the identity has to work across a poster at 18" × 24", a phone screen at 375px wide, a band tee printed at 72 dpi, and a stage backdrop at 12 feet. Most brand systems aren't designed for that kind of resolution range. These have to be.

02 Futures · Tech

Product Futures Workshop

A half-day futures workshop run for a tech company's product team, focused on scenario planning for their category over a 5-year horizon. The format: four plausible futures mapped across two axes of uncertainty, stress-tested against the company's current roadmap. The output wasn't a prediction — it was a set of strategic options and the conditions under which each would become relevant.

SCENARIO MAPThe four futures plotted across the uncertainty axes
WORKSHOP SESSIONTeam working through scenario implications
OUTPUT DOCUMENTStrategic options brief delivered post-workshop
03 Advisory · Hardware

Music Hardware Startup Advisory

Ongoing advisory for an early-stage company building hardware at the intersection of music and consumer electronics. The engagement focuses on product strategy, community-building, and go-to-market for a category where distribution and trust are built through enthusiast communities before mass channels. My background as a bassist and DIY show organizer makes the community dynamics legible in a way that most product advisors miss.

ADVISORY ENGAGEMENTProduct strategy session or hardware prototype review
COMMUNITY MAPPINGAudience and distribution channel analysis
ROADMAP SESSIONProduct prioritization work
